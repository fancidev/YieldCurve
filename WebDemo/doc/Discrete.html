Discrete Model

This is a simple model that discretize the forward curve into a few points and optimize a simple quadratic form.

Divide the horizon $[0, T]$ into $n$ equal periods $\delta = T / n$, and label $0 = \tau_0 < \tau_1 < \cdots < \tau_n = T$. Let $f_i$, $1 \le i \le n$ be the forward rate applied to period $(\tau_{i-1}, \tau_i)$. Let $\vSigma_{(n-1)\times(n-1)}$ be the instantaneous covariance matrix of the first $(n-1)$ forward rates. Let $\mu_i = f_{i+1} - f_i$, $1 \le i \le n-1$ be the (discretized) slope of the forward curve. We optimize the quadratic form $$ \frac{1}{2} \vmu^T \vSigma^{-1} \vmu $$ subject to the usual market rate constraints.

To use the Newton solver, let the state vector be $\vx \triangleq (F_0, F_1, \cdots, F_n)^T$ where $F_k \triangleq -\ln P(\tau_k) = \sum_{i=1}^k f_i \delta$. Then $f_i = (F_i - F_{i-1}) / \delta$ (noting that $F_0 \equiv 0$) and $\mu_i = (F_{i+1} - 2 F_i + F_{i-1}) / \delta$. So $$ \vmu \propto \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 1 \\ & 1 & -2 & 1 \\ & & & \ddots \\ & & & 1 & -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} F_0 \\ F_1 \\ \vdots \\ F_n \end{bmatrix} . $$ Writing $\vmu \triangleq \vL \vx$ where $\vL$ is defined above, the quadratic objective is $\vL^T \vSigma^{-1} \vL$. We then use the Newton solver to fit the model.
